Outlook for July

Projection

If July holds the June pattern — with-trend only, Double Confirmation entries, plan followed 90%+ — a repeat of +5R to +7R is realistic. The upward trend from May into June is the clearest signal yet that the discipline rules are working, not just a single lucky stretch.

Risk to watch

The one counter trend slip in June was also the most expensive trade of the month. If that pattern repeats, it suggests sizing should be reduced specifically when stepping outside the plan, rather than just hoping it doesn't happen again.

05

Suggestions

Track Double Confirmation separately. It's only a few weeks old — tag these trades distinctly to measure its win rate in isolation once the sample size grows.
Add a trend filter view. Sort the journal by With Trend vs Counter Trend so any slip is immediately visible rather than discovered after the fact.
Reduce size, don't fully block, on tempting counter trend setups. Try 0.5% instead of 1% — keeps you honest about temptation without shutting down forward-testing curiosity entirely.
Revisit this dashboard monthly. Ask for an update at the start of each new month so the comparison stays current and the trend stays visible.
06

The bigger picture

Three months of flat losses, then a complete reversal. That's not luck — that's the direct result of cutting counter trend trades and tightening entry criteria. The market didn't change in June. You did.

The +7R month happened because of decisions made in May: reviewing the May 19 trade, identifying it as the benchmark, and building a system around repeating it. That's exactly how real traders compound an edge — not through one lucky month, but by identifying what worked and doing it on purpose, repeatedly.

Stay with the process. The next test isn't beating June — it's proving June wasn't an accident.
